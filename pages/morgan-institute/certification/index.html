The Curriculum

17 Modules. One Integrated Framework.

Each module builds on the last. By the end, you won't just have new tools — you'll have a new way of seeing the patient in front of you.

  • A Whole Systems Approach to Healing
    The philosophical and clinical foundation. Why reductionist models fail chronic patients, and how an integrated framework changes everything.
  • A Patient's Guide to Biological Medicine
    Orientation to the terrain model. Understanding the biological terrain, the milieu, and why the organism's internal environment determines outcomes.
  • Blood Lab Evaluation
    Reading labs the way clinicians should — through the lens of optimal function, not just pathological ranges. Pattern recognition that textbooks don't teach.
  • Darkfield Microscopy & Pleomorphism
    Live blood analysis revealing what standard labs miss. The living terrain under the microscope — microbial pleomorphism, blood ecology, and real-time assessment.
  • EAV Testing
    Electroacupuncture according to Voll. Energetic assessment of organ systems, meridians, and remedy compatibility. A diagnostic bridge between East and West.
  • Medicine Makers: Food as Medicine
    Therapeutic nutrition, kitchen pharmacology, and the clinical use of food. How to prescribe food the way you'd prescribe a remedy — with precision and purpose.
  • Homeopathy & C5 Trituration
    Classical homeopathic prescribing and the deeper practice of C5 trituration — a meditative process that reveals the substance's full healing picture.
  • Gemstone Medicine
    The therapeutic application of gemstone essences and crystalline remedies. Subtle medicine that complements clinical protocols in profound ways.
  • Snake Venom Medicine
    Vanessa's signature work. Homeopathic preparations from snake venoms — Lachesis, Naja, Crotalus, and beyond. Deep constitutional prescribing for autoimmune, neurological, and chronic conditions.
  • Miasms
    The inherited predispositions that shape chronic disease. Understanding miasmatic layers gives you a roadmap for deep, constitutional healing that addresses the root — not just the branch.
  • Homeoprophylaxis
    Homeopathic immunization protocols. An evidence-informed approach to disease prevention that honors the body's innate intelligence.
  • Elements of Classical Chinese Medicine
    Five Element theory, the organ clock, and diagnostic methods that have been refined over millennia. Not the abbreviated version you learned in school — the full classical system.
  • The Eight Extraordinary Vessels
    The deepest layer of the meridian system. Constitutional channels that connect prenatal essence to postnatal function. Clinical applications that most acupuncture programs never cover.
  • Shamanic Healing
    The role of spirit in clinical practice. Ceremony, intention, and the practitioner's own healing as a prerequisite for holding space for others.
  • The Power of Intention
    How the practitioner's consciousness shapes clinical outcomes. The science and the art of bringing presence, purpose, and clear intention into every patient encounter.
  • Practice Building
    How to build a sustainable, referral-based practice doing this work. Not marketing gimmicks — the real infrastructure of a healing practice that serves you and your patients for decades.
  • Clinical Supervision
    Supervised case work with Vanessa. Bring your real patients, present your cases, receive guidance. This is where the framework becomes yours.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Is snake venom homeopathy safe?
Yes. These are homeopathic preparations — highly diluted remedies, not raw venom. Remedies like Lachesis, Naja, and Crotalus Horridus have been part of the homeopathic pharmacopoeia for over 150 years. They are prepared according to established pharmacological standards and are completely safe when prescribed by a trained practitioner.
Is this an online program, or do I need to travel?
The certification is a hybrid model. Most didactic content is delivered through live virtual sessions so you can participate from anywhere. Certain modules will include in-person components — to be confirmed once the curriculum is finalized. We work with each cohort to coordinate schedules and locations.
How does this integrate with my existing practice?
This certification is designed to expand and deepen your existing clinical toolkit — not replace it. Most of our practitioners continue seeing patients throughout the program and begin integrating new approaches from the very first module. Whether you practice acupuncture, naturopathy, functional medicine, or something else entirely, the framework adapts to your work.
How is this different from IFM or other functional medicine certifications?
Functional medicine certifications are excellent at identifying dysfunction through lab work and nutritional interventions. This program goes further — it integrates energetic medicine (Chinese medicine, homeopathy), terrain-based assessment (darkfield microscopy, biological medicine), subtle medicine (gemstones, venom remedies), and the spiritual dimension of healing. It's a whole systems approach that treats the practitioner as much as it treats the patient.
Can international practitioners apply?
Yes. We've trained practitioners from around the world. The virtual components make this accessible regardless of where you're based. For in-person modules, we coordinate travel well in advance. Your licensing requirements will vary by country, and we're happy to discuss how the CBMP designation applies in your jurisdiction.
Are payment plans available?
Yes. We offer flexible payment plans for both the Serpent's Path and the Apprenticeship Program. We believe financial barriers shouldn't prevent the right practitioner from doing this work. During the application process, we'll discuss payment options that work for your situation.
